Littering

Why littering is problem?

Littering pollutes our environment and dramatically reduces the usage, pleasure, and value of our public spaces, giving our neighborhoods a rundown, unappealing appearance that discourages others from using and enjoying them. Litter that is dumped in public places, on the side of the road, or in a bush can wash or blow into streams and rivers, damaging the land, the waterways, and the ocean. Along with aiding in the spread of illness and pests, it also causes significant injury and misery to animals who become entangled in, hurt by, or consume discarded things. Each year, littering costs  towns millions of dollars in trash management and cleanup expenditures that could have been spent for essential facilities or services.

Roadside Littering

Highways are one of the most littered regions, with the most prevalent scattered objects being beverage containers, cigarettes, and food-related goods.

How to Prevent littering?

Check that garbage and recycling bags are properly knotted so that stray papers and other objects do not fall out and cause litter. Educate youngsters on the necessity of appropriate waste disposal. Participate in community cleaning events, such as our spring and fall stream cleanups.
